## 4. Section-by-Section Patterns

### TLDR
- Always a single paragraph
- Key highlights separated by semicolons
- Covers: marketplace/placements performance, notable deals or milestones, product/R&D highlights, team additions, upcoming events
- Reads as a standalone summary â€” an investor could read only this and know the state of the business
- Typical length: 4â€“8 lines

### Marketplace Section
- **Supply side** always comes first, then Demand, then Ops efficiency
- Supply updates cover: candidate acquisition volume, new channels tested, quality improvements
- Demand updates cover: new customers signed, expansion within existing customers, pipeline deals, state expansion
- Ops efficiency covers: process improvements, SOP work, seasonal effects, funnel optimization
- Each sub-section uses a bold label followed by a period, then the content (e.g., "**Supply side (candidates).** We've been growing...")

### R&D / Product Section
- Features are described in terms of their impact on the hiring funnel, not technical implementation
- Often includes context on WHY a feature matters (e.g., "this step is known to be a moment in the hiring funnel that sees huge dropoff")
- Progress on longer-term projects (like the candidate app) is tracked month over month
- AI/agent capabilities are highlighted prominently when relevant

### Other Things Happening
- New team members: always includes their name (underlined/linked), title, and 1-2 sentence background
- Conferences: names the specific conference, location, and asks if investors are attending
- Personal travel or team visits
- Occasionally includes photos (earlier updates had images of the team)

### Asks
- Very specific: names of target organizations, states, or types of connections needed
- Often includes context on why (e.g., "these organizations many times have a longer sales cycle, so the earlier we can talk to them the better")
- Framed warmly, not demandingly
- Sometimes includes named contacts at target organizations
